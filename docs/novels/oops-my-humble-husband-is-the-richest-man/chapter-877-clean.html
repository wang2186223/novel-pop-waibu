Chapter 877

Evelyn Ross and Alexander Vance followed Sienna Sterling back to her home.

Sienna's home was very large.

It was a small estate.

There were many servants.

Evelyn and Sienna rode in one car.

Alexander was arranged to sit with the bodyguards.

The assistant sat in the passenger seat.

He watched the two people chatting happily in the back seat through the rearview mirror.

Evelyn spoke very little.

But Sienna seemed to have found a friend her own age.

She chattered non-stop.

This girl was clearly tough on the outside but soft on the inside.

The arrogance and domineering attitude from their first meeting had completely vanished.

Now she was calling Evelyn "Sister Evelyn" repeatedly.

Evelyn listened to her talk.

While also looking at the outside of the Sterling Family estate.

The last time she came to the Sterling Family.

She was trapped at her sister's adoptive mother's house.

She had never seen the Sterling Family estate properly.

Now, looking around like this.

Evelyn realized.

This city was more developed than the outside world.

It was from the inside out.

Every place exuded a sense of refinement and mechanized advancement.

The vehicles on the road were very obedient to traffic control.

Every building looked newly constructed.

The people on the streets had happy smiles on their faces.

This place was like a paradise.

You could hardly see any beggars on the road.

Everyone had their own job.

Those without jobs could receive social welfare.

Ensuring their most basic livelihood.

There seemed to be no discrimination here.

Except for the Sterling Family being high above.

Everyone else was equal.

A chef and a scientist received the same treatment.

No one looked down on anyone else's profession.

This was somewhat miraculous.

This is what Sienna told Evelyn.

What Evelyn saw on the road.

Were also scenes of mutual courtesy between waiters and customers.

Everything looked very good.

Exceptionally good.

Evelyn observed the streets like this.

Until they arrived at Sienna's home.

When the car stopped in the parking lot.

A servant immediately walked over.

She looked at Sienna.

"Miss, did you have another argument with Miss Scarlett Sterling today?"

"Your father already knows about this."

Sienna immediately pouted.

"Tattling again."

"It's always this trick."

"Can't they change it up?"

She said this in a coquettish tone.

But still obediently got out of the car.

Then she led Evelyn and Alexander into the living room.

Sienna cleared her throat softly.

She said to Evelyn.

"My father is a bit domineering."

"He's also a bit... difficult to get along with."

"Don't say anything later."

Evelyn nodded.

But she didn't really think so in her heart.

An uncle who didn't blame her mother.

Definitely wouldn't be a bad person.

Thinking this.

She immediately saw the middle-aged man sitting on the sofa.

He looked about forty or fifty years old.

His hair was meticulously combed and perfectly neat.

He wore a black tuxedo.

He held a cane in his hand.

Both hands rested on the cane.

He stared sternly at Sienna.

His features were handsome.

But he had deep frown lines on his forehead.

Giving him an imposing, intimidating aura.

Evelyn studied him carefully.

She found he really did bear a four or five out of ten resemblance to her mother, Sophia Ashcroft.

Evelyn's looks didn't resemble Sophia Ashcroft.

She looked more like Mr. Valentine.

So the head of the family only gave her face a brief glance.

Then immediately looked at Sienna.

He then roared angrily.

"Kneel down!"

Evelyn was slightly taken aback.

The next moment.

She saw Sienna pout.

Then reluctantly kneeled down.

The movement was so practiced.

Evelyn was momentarily hesitant.

Did she kneel often?

Sienna's father asked.

"Do you know what mistake you made?"

"Do you know why I'm making you kneel?"

Sienna pursed her lips.

She said disdainfully.

"I know."

"It's just because I offended Scarlett, right?"

"But she started it."

"Why should I take the blame?"

"Dad, even though Aunt is locked up."

"Our family is the direct lineage."

"You don't have to make me yield to her every time, do you?"

The man immediately stood up upon hearing this.

"I make you yield to her."

"Because I don't want to attract unnecessary trouble."

"I'm scolding you today."

"Not because of that either."

Sienna was confused.

"Then why?"

The man directly pointed at Evelyn and Alexander.

"It's because of them!"

"Who are they?"

"Why did you bring them home?"

"You offending Scarlett in your usual ways is one thing."

"These two vagrants offended Scarlett."

"Why did you bring them home?"

Sienna was stunned for a moment.

The next moment.

The man spoke.

"Someone."

"Immediately throw these two vagrants out!"
