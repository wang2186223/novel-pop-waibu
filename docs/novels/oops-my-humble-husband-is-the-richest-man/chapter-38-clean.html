"Care to make a bet?"

"Evelyn, care to make a bet? Whoever finishes their thesis first gets to ask the other for a favor."

"Afraid to lose to me again?"

Julian stared.

"Again?"

Evelyn took out her phone and opened her email.

"I won back then."

Julian looked at the timestamp—she had submitted her thesis the day after their bet!

His pupils constricted suddenly.

He had never even come close to keeping up with her...

His pride made him blurt out.

"What's the bet?"

Evelyn said slowly.

"If Team One submits the solution by Friday, and Dr. Nan doesn't give it to you, you will never interfere with Team One's work again."

She wouldn't stay long, but she wanted to secure her team's future.

Julian sneered.

"If you fail to submit, but Dr. Nan gives me the solution, you will obey my every order from now on."

"Deal."

Julian was puzzled.

"Evelyn, you're talented, but how can you possibly beat Dr. Nan?"

Evelyn smiled ironically.

"Did Dr. Nan really agree to be Vance Group's technical support? Did he say it himself?"

Julian froze.

Evelyn ignored him and returned to the Team One office.

The team was sighing, but they perked up when they saw her.
