"Ancient peasants guessing how the emperor lived."

"They said the emperor farmed with a golden hoe."

"He ate white rice and big steamed buns every day."

"He could finish one bowl and throw away another..."

"Alexander's wife, you know nothing."

"Your birth family has no power or influence."

"If he kicks you out with nothing, no one will back you up!"

Evelyn was about to retort.

A cold voice suddenly came from the doorway.

"Who says she has no one to back her up?!"

Evelyn spun around.

A tall figure strode into the room, bringing a chill with him.

His black wool coat accentuated his broad frame.

His gaze swept the room before settling on Evelyn.

He approached quickly, his voice low. "How is Mrs. Ashcroft?"

Alexander was back.

He had messaged last night that his grandmother was stable, though still unconscious.

That had allowed Evelyn to stay with Sophia without worry.

"She's been discharged," she replied softly. "Don't worry."

"Good."

Alexander turned to Richard and Robert, his voice icy. "My marriage is none of your concern. I am responsible for my own wife. Even without her family's support, she has me to rely on!"

Robert sneered. "Who wants to bother with your affairs? A cold-blooded man like you marries an equally unfilial woman! Your grandmother didn't wake last night. We were all there, except her!"

Alexander explained, "Mrs. Ashcroft was hospitalized. Evelyn was attending to her."

Richard scoffed. "Once married into the Vance family, she is a Vance! A mother she just met? Was she afraid of missing out on an inheritance if the woman died? How shortsighted! Can't she tell who's more important, her own mother or her grandmother?"

Evelyn's fists clenched tightly.

Inheritance?

This was a curse and an insult to Sophia!

Her eyes turned frosty.

"Enough!" Alexander's voice was sharp.

Robert frowned. "Who are you telling to shut up? He's your brother!"

Alexander's tone was dark. "If I hear him insult Mrs. Ashcroft one more time, he can start thinking about how to divide his own inheritance."

His ruthless demeanor made Richard immediately fall silent.

Richard knew his brother meant every word.

Robert slammed his hand on the armrest, about to erupt, when Fiona rushed out, beaming. "The Madam is awake!"

Evelyn and Alexander immediately left the others and hurried to the bedroom.

The elderly lady looked refreshed after her sleep.

She smiled at the doorway and said to Alexander, "You rascal, where have you been?"

Alexander stepped forward. "I took a call."

"I see."

Eleanor took Evelyn's hand. "This must be my daughter-in-law! Little Ye, this brat doesn't know how to treat people right. If he wrongs you, tell me. I'll set him straight! Don't you dare run off in a huff!"

Evelyn was stunned.

She and Alexander exchanged a look, then turned to Eleanor. "Grandmother, who are we?"

"Hey! You rascal, I'm your mother! Why are you calling me grandmother?"

Eleanor swatted Alexander's arm.

Alexander stood frozen.

He stared at his grandmother, his fists slowly clenching.

Eleanor held Evelyn's hand tighter. "Little Ye, don't just stand there. Sit down. You're pregnant... Boy or girl, I'll love this child forever once they're born!"

Alexander's eyes reddened slightly.

Even though she had forgotten him, his grandmother still cared.

Robert approached, sounding relieved. "Mother actually remembers me..."

Richard quickly added, "Of course, Father. You are her only son! She dotes on Alexander only because he's your son..."

A smile appeared on Robert's face.

All these years, the old lady had only had eyes for Alexander, ignoring the main family entirely. He never thought she would still remember him in her confusion.

Robert pushed Alexander aside and leaned closer to the bed. "Mother, how are you feeling?"

Eleanor frowned. "Old man? Aren't you dead? Back from the grave? A zombie?"

Robert's mouth twitched. "Mother, I'm Robert."

"Nonsense!" Eleanor waved her hand, pointing at Alexander. "My son is handsome and dashing. How could it be you, an old codger! You look like trouble!"

Robert fell silent.

Several people in the room stifled laughs, but Evelyn frowned slightly.

The old lady's Alzheimer's was worsening. Her memory had regressed to when Robert was young... She needed medication, fast!

She took out her phone to message the New York contact, but a WhatsApp arrived first: [President Nora, we have arrived at the Vance residence in San Francisco. Delivering the medication immediately!]

Researcher Nora's Chinese transliteration was Nuòlā, and she was called President Nora domestically.

Evelyn breathed a sigh of relief.

The medication would arrive today.

Cecilia spoke softly, "Grandfather, don't be upset. Great-Grandmother is just ill..."

Robert sighed. "I wonder if she'll ever remember me."

His feelings toward his mother were complicated.

His father died early, and his mother raised him alone. The Vance Group started with her hardware business. Later, they disagreed on management philosophy, and she stepped back.

But several company veterans were her old subordinates.

Eleanor held thirty percent of Vance Group's shares and had a group of loyal followers.

Alexander quickly gained control after returning from abroad solely due to her support!

Robert had mixed feelings of love and resentment toward his mother!

He looked at Cecilia. "Is this medication you mentioned really effective?"

Cecilia nodded. "Grandfather, all fifteen specialized drugs developed by Researcher Nora are on the market with excellent feedback. She has been researching this Alzheimer's drug for a long time..."

Her phone suddenly rang.

Cecilia looked down, her face lighting up. "Grandfather, the lab team is at the door. I'll go greet them!"

Cecilia hurried out.

Robert watched her leave, then glanced at Alexander and Evelyn, snorting. "You should be nicer to Cecilia from now on. Especially you, Alexander. She secured this hard-to-get medication for your grandmother. You owe her."

Evelyn laughed coldly. "That's not right. Is Grandmother only Alexander's grandmother? Isn't she Cecilia's great-grandmother too?"

She found it utterly ridiculous.

Robert glared at her. "No manners! How dare you talk back to your elder!"

Richard quickly fanned the flames. "Father, don't be angry. Alexander's wife is just jealous that you favor Cecilia..."

"Evelyn, if you could get your hands on specialized medication like Cecilia, I'd favor you too!" Robert sneered. "But you have no connections, no family background. Can you?"

Evelyn narrowed her eyes. "Who says I can't?"

Before the words faded, Cecilia led the research institute staff through the door.
